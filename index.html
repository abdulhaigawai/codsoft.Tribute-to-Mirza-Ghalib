About Mirza Ghalib

Mirza Asadullah Khan Ghalib (1797–1869) was a legendary poet whose works in Urdu and Persian are revered worldwide. His poetry delves into themes of love, loss, and the complexities of human existence, blending profound wisdom with unparalleled elegance. Ghalib's life was marked by personal struggles, but his art transcended time, influencing generations of poets and readers.

Famous Works

  • Divan-e-Ghalib: A collection of Ghalib’s Urdu ghazals, celebrated for their depth and lyricism.
  • Persian Poetry: His Persian verses exhibit a mastery of language and profound philosophical insights.
  • Letters: Ghalib's letters provide a glimpse into his humor, wit, and the culture of 19th-century India.
